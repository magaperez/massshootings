Basic Gunshot Wound Treatment

Surviving a gunshot wound depends greatly on how quickly a patient gets to a hospital. Ideally, a gunshot wound patient should be on the way to a hospital in an ambulance within 10 minutes of being shot.

The amount of damage depends on the location of the injury and the speed and type of bullet. Gunshot wounds to the head or torso are likely to cause more damage.

Treatment Steps

Direct Pressure

Blood needs to clot in order to start the healing process and stop the bleeding.

Put pressure directly on the wound. If you have some type of gauze, use it.

Elevate Above the Heart

Elevate the wound above the heart. By elevating the wound, you slow the flow of blood.

If the gauze or towel soaks through with blood, add another layer.

Pressure Points

Common Pressure Points:

- Arm between shoulder and elbow

- Groin area along bikini line

- Behind the knee - popliteal artery.

Gunshots wounds that pass through the body without hitting major organs, blood vessels, or bone tend to cause less damage.

Most bleeding can be stopped before the ambulance arrives at the scene.The first step is to control the bleeding

Being shot by a gun is traumatic.

Remember...it is common to have a range of emotions after a traumatic incident.
