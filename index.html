Nearly 300 mass shootings devastated communities across the U.S. in 2019

In spite of this, there is no consensus on the definition of a mass shooting.

The FBI defines “mass shootings” as any incident in which four or more people are shot and killed, excluding the shooter.

Contrary to popular belief,more than 99% of gun deaths in the U.S. are from shootings other than mass shootings.

While mass shootings in public places tend to receive more media coverage, the majority of these shootings occur in private homes.

It is essential to combat the persistent myth that mental illness is a leading factor in mass shootings.

This is unsupported by evidence.
